Futuready Motor Insurance
Tripling conversions by redesigning what users see first

Redesigned a B2B2C motor insurance product page embedded in e-wallet apps. Heatmap data revealed where users abandoned — three targeted interventions drove +230% conversion.

01
Problem
A product page that lost half its audience before they read a word
Futuready sold motor insurance through B2B2C partnerships — embedded inside e-wallet apps like LinkAja. Users got push notifications after paying for fuel at gas stations. Traffic existed. Conversions didn't. 50% of users left before scrolling, the IMEI field cost ~2 minutes of friction, and key product info was buried in accordions.
02
Process
Heatmaps and session recordings showed exactly where the page failed
I ran heatmap analysis and session recordings, then split the data into two segments: users who converted vs. users who didn't. Layered in time-on-page data for granular patterns. Peak traffic was 4PM–9PM weekdays — users checked push notifications later from home, not at the gas station.
Heatmap segmentation — converters vs. non-converters
Final design — product page, benefits detail, and insurance certificate. Template scaled to 5+ product categories.
03
Key Decisions
Three changes that fixed the funnel
Full redesign wasn't an option — too slow, too risky. I picked the three highest-impact interventions based on what the data showed. The redesigned page became the base template across 3 countries and 5+ product categories.
Shipped
Front-loaded product info in first fold
Price, coverage, USPs visible without scrolling.
Tradeoff: Lost standalone promo banner — marketing compromise
Shipped
Surfaced benefits & exceptions
Pulled coverage out of accordions. T&C and Claims stayed collapsed.
Tradeoff: Longer page, but data showed length wasn't the problem
Shipped
Reduced form to 3 fields via partner negotiation
Used session recording data to convince the insurance partner.
Tradeoff: Post-payment data collection adds a second touchpoint
Killed
Full page rebuild with custom illustrations
Too slow for the timeline.
Why killed: Incremental changes shipped faster
Deferred
IMEI lookup automation (API integration)
Engineering capacity wasn't there.
Deferred: Flagged for Phase 2
