Futuready Motor Insurance
Tripling conversions by redesigning what users see first

B2B2C motor insurance embedded in e-wallet apps. Heatmap data showed exactly where users dropped. Three targeted interventions delivered +230% conversion.

Context

Futuready sold motor insurance through B2B2C partnerships embedded in e-wallet apps like LinkAja. Users received push notifications after paying for fuel. Traffic existed. Conversions didn't.

50% of users left before scrolling. The IMEI field cost ~2 minutes of friction. Key product info was buried in accordions. The page was designed as a brochure, not a decision tool.

No full rebuild. Timeline was 3 months, engineering capacity was limited. The brief: find the highest-impact interventions from behavioral data and ship them fast.

I owned research (heatmaps, session recordings), all UX and interaction design, and stakeholder negotiation with the insurance partner to reduce form fields from 5+ to 3. See the process walkthrough below.

Process and reflection
Replace: Problem slide — before-state screenshots. 50% drop before scroll. IMEI field friction (~2 min). Product info buried in accordions. Data from heatmap analysis.
Replace: Research slide — heatmap segmentation, converters vs. non-converters. Peak traffic 4PM-9PM weekdays (users at home, not at the pump). Drop-off concentrated at first fold.
Replace: Direction slide — 3 highest-impact interventions identified from data: front-load product info, surface benefits, reduce form fields. Full rebuild killed (too slow for the timeline).
Replace: Negotiation slide — session recording data used to convince insurance partner to cut form from 5+ fields to 3. Post-payment data collection as the accepted compromise.
Replace: Outcomes slide — +230% conversion, +60% time on page. Template adopted across Indonesia, Romania, Thailand. 5+ product categories using the same base.
Replace: Reflection slide — two-col layout. Left (green dash): used behavioral data to win the partner negotiation, targeted interventions over full rebuild, template thinking from day one. Right (amber dash): no A/B test on individual changes so impact per intervention is unknown, IMEI automation deferred without a committed Phase 2 date.
