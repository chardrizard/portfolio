Context

AIP served 22,000+ independent insurance advisors across 7 Aegon business units: Hypotheek, Pensioen, Leven, Inkomen and more. It was the primary workspace for advisors handling 40+ clients a day.

The existing system showed every BU to every advisor, regardless of WFT license or active portfolio. 85% of interactions landed on 5% of nav links. Search was 3 clicks deep. Context reset on every navigation change.

Brought in as Lead Product Designer to own the full redesign, from research through to handoff, inside a regulated insurance environment with a hard 12-month deadline. See the process walkthrough ↓

The brief was a portal refresh. What it needed was a structural fix: role-based scoping, persistent workflow context, and a landing that reflected each advisor's actual day.

02 — Insight
The interface wasn't too simple. It was relentlessly complete.
The portal treated all advisors identically: one interface, everything visible, regardless of WFT licensing, active BUs, or task context. The problem wasn't missing features. It was absent role scoping.
GA360 analytics Hotjar session recordings Advisor near-interviews Bi-weekly stakeholder sessions Cross-platform journey mapping

"Three distinct advisor personas were using one undifferentiated portal. Every nav item was visible to everyone, every session."

Research synthesis · Q4 2021
03 — Design approach
Three principles: role-based disclosure, persistent context, system-first
Proposed a structural fix, not a refresh. WFT-scoped navigation to cut irrelevant BUs. Persistent search to address the #1 pain point. 90%+ DS adoption to compress the timeline from 18+ to 12 months.
Persistent search bar WFT-scoped navigation Value Stream Dashboard 90%+ DS adoption Stakeholder alignment (7 BUs)

What worked
Killing darlings before engineering touched them
The full-page search bar was cut in testing, not in retrospect. Two concepts validated out with zero engineering spend.
Speaking business, not design
Framing load time as "user retention impact" and role-scoping as "advisor efficiency" got stakeholder sign-off across all 7 BUs. Language was the lever.
DS adoption baked in from day one
90%+ design system reuse wasn't an afterthought. It was in the architecture from the start. That's what took 18+ months to 12.
What I'd do differently
Content managers in the room from Phase 1
Content migration became a mid-project bottleneck. An early audit would have caught it. That's time we didn't have to lose.
Shared terminology with engineers upfront
Documentation investment at the start would have compressed the dev cycle. We figured it out, but not as fast as we could have.
Structured post-launch check-ins
30/60/90 day check-ins would have caught advisor workflow drift before it compounded. We shipped without building in a feedback loop.
