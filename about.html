Origin

Indonesian by birth. Dutch by a series of good decisions.

Grew up in Indonesia, moved to the Netherlands for the opportunity. Tbh a lot of it was other people opening the right doors at the right time.

Before UX, I went through the Apple Developer Academy at BINUS in Jakarta — the first of its kind in Southeast Asia. Spent a year building iOS apps and figuring out that designers who understand how things are made are just more useful.

Every move I've made has been toward whatever's changing fastest. Still is.

The body

Miss a week at the gym and I feel it in my thinking.

Not in a wellness way. More like I get slower and worse at decisions.

So naturally I built a PWA to track it. Started as a spreadsheet. Then Notion. Then a full app with offline support, PR history, and chart views.

My deadlift data is better documented than most enterprise systems I've been paid to fix.

The maker

I over-engineer things nobody asked for. On purpose.

I spot something annoying. I ask what I actually want. Then I build it — usually for hours straight without noticing.

The gym PWA started as a pain point. So did this portfolio. So did a few other things on GitHub that nobody uses but me. I don't really care about the outcome. The problem is what gets me going.

That's either a personality trait or a character flaw. Possibly both.
