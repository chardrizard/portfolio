Who I am

Indonesian-born, Netherlands-based. I grew up in Indonesia and moved to the Netherlands for my studies. That cross-cultural lens shows up in my work — I'm wired to think about how different people experience the same interface.

I'm a Senior UX Designer and Design Systems specialist. I've spent the past several years embedded in complex domains — insurance, healthcare, fintech — designing the systems and flows that most designers avoid. Multi-stakeholder enterprise portals, regulatory-bound workflows, legacy system modernization.

I don't just design screens. I think in systems, constraints, and outcomes. My best work happens when the problem is messy, the stakeholders are many, and the path to clarity isn't obvious.

I speak fluent English and Dutch (B2), and I'm comfortable navigating between design, engineering, and product conversations. I care about craft, but I care more about whether the thing I built actually moved the number it was supposed to move.

What drives me
Systems over screens
I think in components, tokens, and patterns — not pages. A well-built system compounds; a well-built page doesn't.
Outcomes over output
Pretty mockups mean nothing if they don't move a metric. I tie every design decision back to a measurable business outcome.
Niche problem spaces
I gravitate toward the complex, the regulated, the overlooked. Enterprise workflows, compliance flows, multi-stakeholder systems.
Beyond work

When I'm not deep in Figma or wrangling design tokens, I'm usually at the gym — I track my lifts obsessively (and yes, I built a PWA for that). I'm into fitness, cooking Indonesian food for friends, and tinkering with side projects that let me explore the edges of what's possible with code and design.

I'm also a firm believer in building in public and learning by making. This portfolio itself was built with that philosophy — every case study, every component, every interaction is hand-crafted and iterated on with intention.
